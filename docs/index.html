Verification contract

A gene earns its color.

Every verified claim is judged against a fresh-empty baseline under a frozen training contract. Each published DNA record also carries a layered testing ledger: discovery, calibration, seed isolation, held-out controls, lineage breeding, ablations, model and corpus coordinates, and hash-chain checks are listed separately. Failed, experimental, and unperformed checks remain visible. Incremental inherited-baseline results remain explicitly experimental, cross-corpus claims use thresholds frozen separately for each corpus, and joint scaling is displayed by exact parameter counts and corpus-token budgets; nominal targets remain internal protocol coordinates.

Inspect the public evidence catalog ↗
  1. 01

    Begin empty

    Search starts from genesis_empty. Historical model DNA is excluded from source discovery.

  2. 02

    Freeze the race

    Corpus, optimizer, schedule, budget, evaluation interval, and seed pairing remain fixed.

  3. 03

    Measure convergence

    Legacy claims use linearly interpolated steps to PPL 7; cross-corpus claims use corpus-specific frozen thresholds and show unsmoothed geometric-mean PPL curves with the observed five-seed min–max range. Fixed-token parameter-count studies remain a separate reference. Joint studies consume nested, continuous corpus prefixes once and plot progress as a fraction of that independent prefix.

  4. 04

    Cross the gate

    Verified means at least 10% fewer ratio-of-means tokens versus fresh empty DNA across five held-out pairs, with every run hitting threshold. Cross-corpus and joint cells also require at least 4/5 positive pairs and non-regressing log-PPL excess AUC.

  5. 05

    Publish every check

    The evidence ledger distinguishes search from held-out validation, enumerates fresh-empty and inherited-parent controls, records breeding and ablation arms, and names any control that was not run. A missing experiment is never presented as a passed result.
